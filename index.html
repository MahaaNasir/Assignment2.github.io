Protected areas and its importance


One touch of nature makes the whole world kin.
~ William Shakespeare





Locations that are protected because of their acknowledged natural, ecological, or
cultural importance are known as protected areas or conservation areas.

They are crucial for maintaining biodiversity as well as providing key ecosystem services,
such as safeguarding watersheds, soils and protecting populated areas from natural disasters.

There are many types of protected areas. National parks, Sanctuaries, Conservation
Reserves, and Community Reserves are the four types of protected areas.

To know more about the protected areas, please select one of the
following categories.

Sanctuaries


In protected natural areas, also called sanctuaries there is little human interaction with the environment. They support a wide variety of plants and animals in forests, lakes, rivers, streams, and wetlands.


National parks


The purpose of national parks, which are places under federal protection, is to conserve the environment.


Conservation Reserves


The land that the government has set aside as a reserve and prohibited access to ensure the safety of wildlife.


Community Reserves


A privately held plot of land that contains wildlife resources, either by a single person or the entire community.
